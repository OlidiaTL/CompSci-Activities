PART 2: ROBOTICS

üìå KEY DEFINITION: The word "robot" comes from the Czech word "robota" meaning "forced labour". Robotics is a branch of computer science that brings together the design, construction, and operation of robots.

2.1 The Three Characteristics of a Robot

1. ABILITY TO SENSE SURROUNDINGS:

  • Uses sensors (light, pressure, temperature, acoustic)
  • Recognizes immediate environment
  • Determines size, shape, weight, temperature of objects
  • All sensor data sent to microprocessor/computer

2. DEGREE OF MOVEMENT:

  • Uses wheels, cogs, pistons, gears
  • Can turn, twist, move, grip, lift
  • Contains motors, hydraulic pipes, actuators, circuit boards
  • Can use different end effectors (welding, spraying, cutting, lifting)

3. PROGRAMMABLE:

  • Has a 'brain' called a controller
  • Controller determines actions to perform tasks
  • Relies on data from sensors or cameras
  • Can be programmed for specific tasks

PART 3: ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI)

üìå KEY DEFINITION: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science dealing with the simulation of intelligent human behaviour by a computer. It replicates cognitive functions like reasoning, learning, and understanding.

3.1 Three Categories of AI

Category Description
Narrow AI Machine has SUPERIOR performance in ONE specific task
General AI Machine has SIMILAR performance to humans in a specific task
Strong AI Machine has SUPERIOR performance in MANY tasks

3.2 Characteristics of AI

AI is a collection of rules and data, with the ability to reason, learn, and adapt to external stimuli.

REASONING: The ability to draw reasoned conclusions based on given data/situations. Uses deductive reasoning to build up facts into rules that can be applied to other problems.

LEARNING & ADAPTING: The AI system learns from sequences of steps and applies knowledge to new situations. Can quickly discern patterns and make predictions.

3.3 Examples of AI

  • News generation based on live feeds
  • Smart home devices (Amazon Alexa, Google Now, Apple Siri, Microsoft Cortana)
  • Chatbots that interact through instant messaging

PART 4: EXPERT SYSTEMS & MACHINE LEARNING

4.1 Expert Systems

üìå DEFINITION: An expert system is a computer system that mimics human decision-making ability. It uses AI to simulate the judgement and behaviour of a human or organization with expert knowledge.

How Expert Systems Work:

They ask a series of questions, analyze answers, and provide a diagnosis or conclusion with a percentage probability of accuracy.

Example: A medical expert system might analyze symptoms (impaired vision, lack of coordination, weak muscles, slurred speech, worked in paint factory) and diagnose mercury poisoning.

4.2 Applications of Expert Systems

  • Oil and mineral prospecting
  • Medical diagnosis
  • Fault diagnostics in equipment
  • Tax and financial calculations
  • Strategy games (chess)
  • Logistics and delivery routing
  • Identification of plants, animals, compounds

4.3 Advantages and Disadvantages

‚úÖ ADVANTAGES

  • High level of expertise
  • High accuracy
  • Consistent results
  • Stores vast amounts of information
  • Traceable logical solutions
  • Multiple expertise possible
  • Very fast response times
  • Unbiased reporting
  • Shows probability of solutions

‚ùå DISADVANTAGES

  • Users need considerable training
  • Very high setup & maintenance costs
  • May give "cold" responses
  • Only as good as data entered
  • Users may assume they're infallible

4.4 Machine Learning

üìå DEFINITION: Machine learning is the science of training computers with sample data so they can make predictions about new unseen data, without needing to be specifically programmed for the new data.
